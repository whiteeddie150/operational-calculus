Tailboard Safety Briefing

Date: February 25, 2026
Topic: Hand and Finger Safety

---

Situation

Our hands are constantly exposed during tool use, material handling,
equipment operation, and working around pinch points. Many tasks place
fingers between moving parts, sharp edges, or heavy loads.

---

Why It Matters

Hand and finger injuries are among the most common workplace injuries and
can result in fractures, lacerations, amputations, and long-term loss of
function. Most occur during routine tasks when positioning and awareness
slip for just a moment.

---

Operational Hazards

- Pinch points between tools, equipment, and materials
- Hands placed under suspended or shifting loads
- Sharp edges on metal, cable, and hardware
- Removing gloves for dexterity and exposing skin to hazards
- Rushing or repositioning without clear hand placement

---

Control Measures

- Identify pinch points before placing hands near moving parts
- Keep hands out of the line of fire and never under suspended loads
- Use tools, not hands, to guide or position materials
- Wear task-appropriate gloves and inspect them for damage
- Communicate clearly during team lifts or equipment movement
- Slow down during repositioning and final placement

---

Relevant Statistics and Sources

Hand injuries are among the most frequently reported workplace injuries
across industries. OSHA identifies pinch points and caught-between hazards
as leading causes of serious hand injuries.

OSHA – Machine Guarding & Pinch Point Hazards:
https://www.osha.gov/machine-guarding

National Safety Council – Injury Facts: https://injuryfacts.nsc.org

---

Field Safety Reminder

You only get one set of hands. Slow down, watch your placement, and keep
them out of the line of fire.
