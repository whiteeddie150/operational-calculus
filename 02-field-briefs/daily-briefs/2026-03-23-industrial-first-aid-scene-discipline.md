Tailboard Safety Briefing

Topic: Industrial First Aid – Scene Discipline and Early Decisions

---

Situation

In an industrial environment, first aid is often delivered in uncontrolled
settings, around energized equipment, moving machinery, or unstable
conditions. Even for trained providers, the hazard environment can be more
dangerous than the injury itself.

---

Why It Matters

For medically trained crews, the risk is not lack of skill, it is losing
scene discipline. NIOSH and OSHA both emphasize that responders must
ensure scene safety before patient care, as secondary injuries to rescuers
are a known risk in industrial incidents.

---

Operational Hazards

• Entering a hazard zone without confirming it is controlled
• Focusing on patient care while ignoring ongoing risks (energy, movement,
atmosphere)
• Incomplete size-up due to urgency or familiarity
• Multiple responders converging without coordination
• Loss of accountability or scene control

---

Control Measures

• Perform a deliberate scene size-up before contact
• Confirm hazards are controlled (energy isolation, equipment stopped,
area secured)
• Establish scene control, one lead, clear roles
• Slow down initial actions to maintain awareness
• Treat scene safety as part of patient care, not separate from it

---

Relevant Federal Safety Guidance

OSHA requires that emergency response be conducted only after hazards are
assessed and controlled to protect responders.
NIOSH investigations have repeatedly shown that secondary injuries occur
when responders enter unsafe scenes without full hazard control.

---

Supporting Language from PG&E Governing Documents

PG&E emphasizes that safety is the highest priority and that employees are
accountable for protecting themselves, coworkers, and the public through
disciplined safe work practices.

---

Supporting Language from IBEW 1245 Physical Agreement

The Company and Union affirm that the safety of employees, contractors,
and the public is the highest priority and commit to maintaining safe

---

Field Safety Reminder

You can replace a patient, you cannot replace a rescuer. Control the scene
before you commit to care.

---

2-Sentence Safety Message

Industrial first aid starts with scene control, not patient contact. Slow
down, size up, and make sure the hazard is controlled before committing to
care.

---

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Scene safety comes before patient care
• Confirm hazards are controlled before entry
• Perform a full size-up, even if the injury looks minor
• Establish one lead and clear roles
• Do not become the second patient
