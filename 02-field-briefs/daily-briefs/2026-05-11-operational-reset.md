**Sent:** 2026-05-11

Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Operational Reset – Regain Control Before the Week Speeds Up

Situation
A lot of bad Mondays start before the job even begins.

People come into the week carrying:
- unfinished thoughts
- distractions
- changing priorities
- stress from previous work
- pressure to regain tempo quickly

When that happens, the work can start moving faster than our awareness.

Why It Matters
Research in aviation, human performance, medicine, and industrial
operations shows that scattered attention, interruption carryover, and
cognitive overload reduce:
- situational awareness
- decision quality
- verification
- error detection

The brain does not automatically reset between stressors, interruptions, or
operational shifts.

Operational Risk
When people start the week mentally scattered:
- assumptions increase
- verification decreases
- shortcuts appear
- communication degrades
- drift accelerates

Most of the time, the problem is not lack of skill.

The problem is:
- degraded awareness
- rushed tempo
- operating before regaining control

Connections to Previous Briefs
We've talked before about:
- drift
- interruption recovery
- cognitive bias
- questioning attitude
- verification vs assumption
- weak signals

Operational reset is what helps prevent those problems from stacking
together.

An interruption, rushed start, or overloaded schedule creates conditions
where:
- assumptions replace verification
- weak signals get missed
- drift accelerates
- controls degrade

Where It Shows Up

In the Field
- rushing setup
- incomplete pre-job focus
- restarting work too quickly
- multitasking critical steps
- scattered communication

In the Office
- jumping between priorities
- overloaded email/call traffic
- fragmented planning
- unfinished tasks carrying into new work

Behavioral Warning Signs
- rushing to regain tempo
- "I'll catch up as I go"
- forgetting small details
- increased frustration
- skipping verification
- operating on autopilot

Operational Strength
Strong crews deliberately reset before accelerating:
- slow down briefly
- reassess conditions
- reestablish priorities
- verify controls
- regain situational awareness

They don't let tempo outrun awareness.

Connection to PG&E Standards
PG&E expects:
- a questioning attitude
- verification over assumption
- stop when unsure

Operational reset supports all three.

Field Safety Reminder
Before the work starts moving fast, make sure you're actually in control of
it.

2-Sentence Safety Message
The brain does not automatically reset between stressors, interruptions, or
operational shifts. Slow down long enough to regain awareness before
accelerating into the work.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Slow down before accelerating
- Don't assume you're mentally reset
- Verify before regaining tempo
- Watch for scattered attention
- Regain control before continuing
