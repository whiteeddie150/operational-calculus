Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Speaking Up is a Safety Control

Situation
We spend a lot of time talking about tools, equipment, and physical
hazards. One of the biggest factors in whether we catch a problem early is
whether people actually speak up when something doesn't look right.

Why It Matters
Most incidents don't happen because nobody saw the problem. They happen
because someone saw something and didn't say anything.
A strong crew isn't the one that never has issues, it's the one where
people speak up early and fix them before they turn into something bigger.

How This Actually Works in the Field

1. Be Part of the Crew
If someone feels like an outsider, they won't speak up.
We treat everyone like they belong here, because the quiet guy might be the
one who sees the problem first.

2. Ask the Question
If something doesn't make sense, ask.
Guessing leads to mistakes. Questions fix problems early.

3. Say What You See
If you notice something off, say it.
You don't need to be the senior guy to call out a hazard.

4. Speak Up When It Matters
If something doesn't look right, stop it.
This is where accidents get prevented, when someone is willing to say "hold
up" before it goes wrong.

Operational Hazards
- Staying quiet when something feels off
- Assuming someone else will say something
- Not questioning a plan that doesn't look right
- Letting small issues slide
- Waiting too long to act

Control Measures
- Speak up early, don't wait
- Ask questions instead of guessing
- Call out hazards as soon as you see them
- Back each other when someone raises a concern
- Use stop work when control is unclear

Stop Work and Communication
If you see it and don't say it, the hazard stays in play.
Speaking up is part of doing the job safely.

Field Safety Reminder
The problem you don't say anything about is the one that gets through.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Strong crews speak up early and fix problems before they grow. If something
doesn't look right, say it.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Don't stay quiet, speak up
- Ask when something doesn't make sense
- Call out what you see
- Back each other up
- Stop it early, don't wait
