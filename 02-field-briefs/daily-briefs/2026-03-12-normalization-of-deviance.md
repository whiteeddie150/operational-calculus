Tailboard Safety Briefing

Topic: Normalization of Deviance

---

Situation

Over time, small deviations from procedures or safety margins can begin to
feel normal if no incident occurs. Workers may gradually accept unsafe
conditions simply because they have not caused problems in the past.

---

Why It Matters

Normalization of deviance occurs when unsafe practices slowly become
accepted as normal. This concept was identified during investigations of
the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster, where repeated warning signs were
accepted because earlier launches had not resulted in failure. When
deviations from standards become routine, the likelihood of a serious
incident increases.

---

Operational Hazards

• Accepting shortcuts because they worked before
• Ignoring minor equipment issues
• Skipping procedural steps during routine work
• Working around hazards instead of correcting them
• Assuming familiar tasks are automatically safe

---

Control Measures

• Follow established procedures every time
• Address hazards instead of working around them
• Stop work if conditions differ from the plan
• Encourage coworkers to challenge unsafe practices
• Report hazards and deviations immediately

---

Relevant Federal Safety Guidance

Effective safety programs rely on identifying hazards and correcting them
before they become accepted risks. Workplace safety organizations also
emphasize that repeated exposure to hazards without incidents can lead
workers to underestimate risk.

---

---

Field Safety Reminder

Just because something worked yesterday does not mean it is safe today.
Address hazards before they become accepted practice.

---

2-Sentence Safety Message

Normalization of deviance happens when small safety shortcuts slowly become
accepted because nothing bad happened before. Follow procedures every time
and address hazards before they become routine.

---

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Do not accept unsafe shortcuts
• Address hazards instead of working around them
• Follow procedures every time
• Speak up about unsafe conditions
• Do not assume familiar tasks are automatically safe
