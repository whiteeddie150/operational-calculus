PPE Cross-Mapped to PG&E Control Language

1. Hierarchy of Controls
PPE is the last line of defense, not the primary control.
If PPE is your only control, you're already exposed.

2. Critical / Essential Controls
PPE is often the final barrier preventing serious injury.
If that piece of PPE is compromised, the control is compromised.

3. Verify Controls Before Work
PPE must be present, functional, and properly worn.
If you didn't check it, you didn't verify the control.

4. Stop Work Triggers
Missing, damaged, or improperly used PPE = failed control.
If your PPE isn't right, the job isn't right.

5. Questioning Attitude
Do not assume PPE is good, verify it.
Don't assume your gear is good, know it is.

6. Human Performance / Error Reduction
Familiarity leads to skipped checks and assumptions.
Most gear failures aren't bad luck, they're missed checks.

7. SIF Prevention
PPE is often the last barrier before serious injury.
When everything else fails, PPE is what's left.

Summary
PPE is not separate from the system, it is a control.
If it's not checked, not worn, or not working, the control isn't there.

Key Line
If the PPE fails, the control fails.
