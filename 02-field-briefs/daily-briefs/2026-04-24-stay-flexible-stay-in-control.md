Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Positive Friday – Stay Flexible, Stay in Control

Situation
This week we've talked about task fixation, getting locked into the job,
and missing what's changing around us. These aren't uncommon mistakes,
they're normal human behavior under focus and workload.

Why It Matters
Research shows that when we focus hard on a task, our attention naturally
narrows and we tend to stick with the plan, even when conditions change.
Strong performance isn't just about staying focused, it's about knowing
when to adjust.

What This Looks Like in the Field
- You're focused on the job and stop scanning your surroundings
- Something changes, but you keep going because you're almost done
- You notice something feels off, but don't step back

This is called cognitive lockup, staying committed to the task after the
situation has changed.

Operational Strength
Strong crews don't just work hard, they adapt:
- They look up and stay aware of the full environment
- They recognize when focus becomes tunnel vision
- They step back when conditions change
- They adjust the plan instead of forcing it

Adaptive Resilience
Being tough is pushing through.
Being effective is knowing when to change.

Control and Decision Making
If conditions change and we don't adjust, we lose control.
Control comes from recognizing change early and responding to it.

Field Safety Reminder
The job doesn't get safer just because you're close to finishing it. Stay
aware and stay flexible all the way through.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Stay focused on the task, but don't lose awareness of what's changing
around you. Strong crews adjust early and stay in control.

Adaptive Resilience: keep you brain engaged in your work allowing for
change given evolving conditions. Trusting your intuition from your years
of threat assessment in the fire service

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Don't get locked into the task
- Look up and scan often
- Reassess when conditions change
- Adjust the plan when needed
- Finish strong with awareness
