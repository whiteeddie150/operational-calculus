MORNING SAFETY BRIEF

Topic: Finish Strong

Supporting PG&E Virtue: Owners

Situation

As the workday progresses, our focus naturally shifts toward completing
tasks, loading equipment, returning to vehicles, and getting home. The
challenge is that the final portion of the workday often occurs when
fatigue has had the most time to accumulate.

Why It Matters

NIOSH research has found that fatigue can reduce:

• Attention
• Concentration
• Reaction time
• Memory
• Decision-making

Importantly, performance often declines before people recognize the decline
themselves.

In other words:

"Feeling fine is not always the same as performing at your best."

The Science

Fatigue is not simply being tired.

Fatigue is a reduction in capability.

As fatigue accumulates, workers may experience:

• Slower reactions
• Reduced situational awareness
• Increased errors
• Poorer risk assessment

Research also shows that people often underestimate the effects fatigue is
having on their performance. This is one reason fatigue contributes to
workplace incidents across transportation, emergency services, utilities,
and construction.

Operational Awareness

At the start of the shift, most people are actively evaluating:

• Hazards
• Terrain
• Equipment
• Work conditions

By the end of the shift, attention often begins shifting toward:

• Finishing the task
• Loading up
• The drive home
• Tomorrow's work

The work may not be over, but mentally we can begin transitioning away from
it.

Questioning Attitude

As the day winds down, ask:

• Has my attention changed?
• Am I rushing to finish?
• Am I making the same quality decisions I made this morning?
• Have I become less aware of my surroundings?
• Has fatigue affected my performance?

PG&E Doctrine Alignment

PG&E's Questioning Attitude asks us to prefer facts over assumptions.

Don't assume you are performing the same way at 1600 hours that you were at
0800 hours.

Check the facts.

Pay attention to your performance.

Pay attention to your awareness.

Supporting PG&E Virtue

Owners

Owners take responsibility for the quality of their work from the first
task of the day to the last.

The safest operators understand that the job isn't complete until the
equipment is secured, the vehicle is parked, and everyone gets home safely.

Safety Reminder

Fatigue does not usually announce itself.

It often appears as:

• Missed details
• Rushed decisions
• Reduced awareness
• Small mistakes

The final task of the day deserves the same attention as the first.

2-Sentence Safety Message

Fatigue is a reduction in capability, not simply a feeling of being tired.
Finish the day with the same level of attention and discipline you brought
to the start because fatigue often affects performance before we recognize
it.

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Monitor your performance, not just your energy level
• Avoid rushing to finish tasks
• Stay engaged during cleanup and demobilization
• Recognize that fatigue can affect judgment and awareness
• Finish strong, the work isn't over until everyone gets home

Closing Thought

Fatigue is rarely a dramatic event. More often, it appears as small changes
in awareness, judgment, and performance. Finish strong because the last
task of the day deserves the same attention as the first.
