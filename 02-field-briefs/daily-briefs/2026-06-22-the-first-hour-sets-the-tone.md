MORNING SAFETY BRIEF

Topic: The First Hour Sets the Tone

Supporting PG&E Virtue: Owners

Situation

Mondays often begin with equipment checks, travel, work planning, crew
discussions, and preparation for the week ahead.

These activities may seem routine, but they establish the foundation for
everything that follows.

The first hour of the day is often where we set the conditions for success
or failure.

Why It Matters

Research in human performance and high reliability organizations shows that
small issues identified early are easier to correct than problems
discovered later.

Missed equipment deficiencies, incomplete planning, poor communication, and
overlooked hazards often become larger problems as the workday progresses.

A few extra minutes invested early can prevent hours of difficulty later.

The Science

Human factors researchers have long recognized that people perform better
when they establish a deliberate rhythm before beginning complex or
hazardous work.

Pilots use preflight inspections.

Firefighters perform apparatus checks.

Utility crews inspect vehicles, tools, and equipment.

These practices do not eliminate risk.

They help identify problems while options still exist.

Operational Awareness

Before beginning work today, ask:

• Is my equipment ready?
• Is my vehicle ready?
• Do I understand today's assignment?
• What has changed since the last time I performed this work?
• Is there anything I am assuming rather than verifying?

Questioning Attitude

The beginning of the day is an opportunity to replace assumptions with
facts.

Don't assume equipment is ready because it was yesterday.

Don't assume conditions are unchanged because they usually are.

Verify.

Supporting PG&E Virtue

Owners

Owners establish conditions for success before the work begins.

Preparation is not separate from the job.

Preparation is part of the job.

Safety Reminder

Many incidents begin with something small that was missed early.

The first hour provides an opportunity to identify and correct those issues
while the cost of correction is still low.

2-Sentence Safety Message

The safest workdays rarely happen by accident. They are often the result of
deliberate preparation, verification, and communication that occur before
the most demanding work begins.

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Conduct deliberate equipment checks
• Verify assumptions with facts
• Review today's work plan
• Identify changes from previous workdays
• Use the first hour to establish a strong operational rhythm

Closing Thought

The first hour may be the least exciting part of the day, but it often has
the greatest influence on how the rest of the day unfolds. Start strong,
verify the basics, and set yourself up for success.
