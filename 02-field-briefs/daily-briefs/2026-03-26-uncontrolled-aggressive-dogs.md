Morning Safety Brief

Topic: Uncontrolled / Aggressive Dogs

---

Situation

Crews routinely enter yards, easements, and customer properties where dogs
may be present. Animals can be unrestrained, territorial, or not visible
until you are already in the work area.

---

Why It Matters

Dog attacks cause thousands of work-related injuries every year,
particularly to field personnel entering residential areas. The United
States Postal Service reports over 5,800 dog bite incidents in 2023
involving delivery workers, many occurring at the point of entry to a
property.
OSHA requires employers to protect workers from recognized hazards, which
includes animal attacks under the General Duty Clause.

---

Operational Hazards

• Entering a property without confirming animals are secured
• Dogs not visible until inside the work area
• Territorial behavior near homes, gates, or equipment
• Homeowners stating "the dog is friendly"
• Distraction while focusing on the task instead of surroundings

---

Control Measures

• Stop at the entry point and assess for animals before entering
• Request the owner secure the dog before work begins
• Do not turn your back on a loose or aggressive dog
• Maintain a barrier (gate, truck, equipment) when possible
• Leave the area if the animal is not controlled

---

Relevant Federal Safety Guidance

The United States Postal Service documents thousands of dog attacks
annually on workers, reinforcing that animal encounters are a predictable
occupational hazard.
OSHA requires employers to provide a workplace free from recognized
hazards, which includes animal-related risks.

---

Supporting Language from PG&E Governing Documents

PG&E states that "nothing is more important to us than safety" and
emphasizes employee accountability to protect themselves, coworkers, and
the public through safe work practices.
Customers are expected to secure animals before utility work begins,
reinforcing hazard control prior to task execution.

---

Supporting Language from IBEW 1245 Physical Agreement

The Company and Union affirm that the safety of employees, contractors,
and the public is the highest priority and commit to maintaining safe
working conditions through hazard recognition and control.

---

Field Safety Reminder

If the dog controls the space, you don't. Do not enter until the hazard is
controlled.

---

2-Sentence Safety Message

Uncontrolled dogs are a known hazard for field crews, and most bites happen
when entering a property. If the animal is not secured, do not enter and
request control before work begins.

---

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Stop and assess before entering any property
• Do not trust "the dog is friendly"
• Request animals be secured before work
• Maintain a barrier if a dog is present
• Leave the area if the hazard is not controlled
