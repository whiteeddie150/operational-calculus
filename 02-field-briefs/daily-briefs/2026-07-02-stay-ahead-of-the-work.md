MORNING SAFETY BRIEF

Topic: Stay Ahead of the Work

Supporting PG&E Virtue: Owners

Situation

Every job is already unfolding before we fully enter it.

The terrain, weather, tools, equipment, traffic, fatigue, vegetation,
access, and schedule all create conditions around the work.

Those conditions are not static.

They change as the day moves forward.

Why It Matters

Safety is not just filling out the required forms or completing the
required apps.

Those tools matter only if they help us understand the work better.

The real safety question is:

Are we still ahead of the work, or is the work starting to get ahead of us?

The Concept

Good operators do more than identify hazards.

They understand the process they are entering.

They recognize the constraints around that process.

They compare what they expected to see with what is actually happening.

Then they adapt before options disappear.

Field Application

Before and during work today, ask:

• What process are we entering?
• What constraints are present?
• What should we expect to see?
• What is changing?
• Are our options increasing or shrinking?
• What small adjustment keeps us ahead of the job?

Relevant Thought Leaders

Jens Rasmussen's work reminds us that workers operate under competing
pressures, including production, workload, and safety.

Kim Vicente's Cognitive Work Analysis emphasizes understanding the
constraints built into the work system.

John Flach's work on expertise points toward attunement, meaning skilled
operators learn to recognize what reality is telling them.

Beth Crandall's research on expert knowledge shows that experienced workers
often rely on cues and patterns that are not always written in procedures.

Atul Gawande's work on checklists reminds us that tools only help when they
improve real-world performance, not when they become the work itself.

Questioning Attitude

PG&E's Questioning Attitude fits directly here.

Do not assume the app, the briefing, or yesterday's experience means
today's work is understood.

Ask what has changed.

Ask what the work is becoming.

Ask whether the plan still fits the conditions.

Supporting PG&E Virtue

Owners

Owners do not just complete the safety process.

Owners use the safety process to improve the work.

Owners stay ahead of the job.

Safety Reminder

Safety paperwork should support safety thinking.

If the form is complete but the crew has not identified what is changing,
what is constrained, or where options are narrowing, the safety work is not
complete.

2-Sentence Safety Message

The safest crews are usually the ones that stay ahead of the work. Use the
required safety process to understand the job, recognize constraints, and
make small adjustments before the work starts making decisions for you.

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Know the process you are entering
• Recognize the constraints around the work
• Compare expectations to actual conditions
• Watch for shrinking options
• Stay ahead of the job, not just compliant with the form

Closing Thought

The goal is not to make safety harder.

The goal is to make the work go better.

A good safety process should help us stay ahead of the work.
