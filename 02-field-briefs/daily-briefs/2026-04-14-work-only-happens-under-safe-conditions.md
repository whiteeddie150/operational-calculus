Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Work Only Happens Under Safe Conditions

Situation
Every job we perform is based on the assumption that hazards are identified
and controlled before work begins. As conditions change in the field, that
assumption can become invalid without being clearly recognized.

Why It Matters
California law requires that employers provide a safe and healthful
workplace and that hazards are identified and corrected before work
proceeds. If hazards are not controlled, the work no longer meets required
safety conditions.
This means the job has changed, even if the task itself has not.

Operational Hazards
- Continuing work when conditions have changed
- Uncontrolled hazards in the work area
- Delayed recognition of unsafe conditions
- Pressure to complete work despite changing risk
- Operating without clear hazard control

Control Measures
- Confirm hazards are identified and controlled before starting work
- Reassess conditions continuously during the job
- Stop work if hazards are not controlled
- Communicate changes in conditions immediately
- Resume work only when conditions are brought back under control

Relevant Legal and Safety Guidance
California Labor Code Section 6400 – Employer responsibility to provide a

Supporting Language from IBEW 1245 Physical Agreement
The Company and Union affirm that the safety of employees, contractors, and

are shared responsibilities.

Field Safety Reminder
If hazards are not controlled, the job is not ready to be done.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Work only happens under safe conditions. If the hazard isn't controlled,
the job has changed and must be reassessed.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Identify and control hazards before starting work
- Reassess when conditions change
- Stop work if hazards are not controlled
- Communicate changes immediately
- Resume only when conditions are safe
