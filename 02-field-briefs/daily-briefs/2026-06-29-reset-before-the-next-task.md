MORNING SAFETY BRIEF

Topic: Reset Before the Next Task

Supporting PG&E Virtue: Owners

Situation

Today's work will likely involve multiple locations, equipment changes,
vehicle movements, and different tasks.

Each transition creates an opportunity to either reset your attention or
carry the previous task into the next one.

Why It Matters

Our minds naturally stay focused on what we just completed.

That makes it easy to miss what has changed.

Many incidents occur not because people lack experience, but because they
begin a new task with the mindset of the previous one.

The Science

Human factors research shows that transitions are periods of increased
cognitive demand.

Changing tools, changing locations, or changing objectives requires the
brain to build a new mental picture of the work.

If we fail to make that reset, we may overlook hazards that weren't present
just moments before.

Operational Awareness

Before starting each new task today, pause for a few seconds and ask:

• What has changed?
• What hazards are different?
• What equipment am I using now?
• What is my first priority?
• What should I expect to see?

That brief pause helps establish a new baseline for the task ahead.

Questioning Attitude

Don't let yesterday's assumptions, or even the last task's assumptions,
become today's reality.

Every task deserves a fresh look.

Supporting PG&E Virtue

Owners

Owners don't simply complete work.

They deliberately prepare for the next piece of work.

Small resets help prevent small mistakes from becoming larger problems.

Field Application

Today:

• Pause before each new task.
• Verify your equipment.
• Scan for changing conditions.
• Build a new mental picture.
• Begin deliberately.

Safety Reminder

Professional operators don't just finish one task well.

They intentionally prepare for the next one.

2-Sentence Safety Message

Transitions are when our attention is most vulnerable. Taking a few seconds
to mentally reset before beginning the next task helps us recognize hazards
that routine can easily hide.

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Pause before starting a new task
• Ask what has changed
• Verify tools and PPE
• Build a fresh mental picture
• Start deliberately, not automatically

Closing Thought

The safest workers don't carry yesterday's assumptions into today's work.
They reset their attention every time the work changes.
