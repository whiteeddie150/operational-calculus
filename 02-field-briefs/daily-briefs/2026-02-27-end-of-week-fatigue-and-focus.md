Tailboard Safety Briefing

Date: Friday, February 27, 2026
Topic: End-of-Week Fatigue and Focus

---

Situation

It is the end of the workweek. Cumulative physical fatigue, early starts,
long hours, and mental load can reduce focus and increase the likelihood
of mistakes during routine tasks.

---

Why It Matters

Fatigue slows reaction time, impairs judgment, and reduces situational
awareness. Studies show fatigue can affect performance similarly to
impairment, increasing the risk of incidents, equipment damage, and
injuries.

---

Operational Hazards

• Slower reaction time and reduced hazard recognition
• Rushing to finish tasks before the weekend
• Reduced communication and missed details
• Overconfidence during familiar end-of-week routines
• Increased risk of shortcuts to "wrap things up"

---

Control Measures

• Slow down and maintain deliberate pace through the end of shift
• Double-check work, especially final steps and equipment securement
• Communicate clearly during transitions and task completion
• Take short breaks to reset focus if fatigue is setting in
• Speak up if you or a teammate appears fatigued or distracted

---

Relevant Statistics from Federal Data

- The National Safety Council identifies fatigue as a major contributor to
workplace incidents and reduced cognitive performance.
- Fatigue-related impairment can significantly increase error rates and
injury risk.

2-Minute Narrative (Condensed)

Today's focus is end-of-week fatigue. After a full week of physical and
mental demands, it is normal for focus and energy to drop. That is when
small mistakes happen, rushing to finish, missing a step, or overlooking a
hazard.

Fatigue slows reaction time and affects judgment. It can make routine tasks
more dangerous because attention drifts and communication drops off.

The controls are simple. Slow down. Double-check your work. Communicate
clearly. Take a short reset if you need it. Watch out for each other and
speak up if someone looks fatigued.

Finish the week focused and deliberate. The goal is for everyone to go home
safe and ready for the weekend.
