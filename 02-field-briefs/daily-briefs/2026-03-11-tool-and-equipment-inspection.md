Tailboard Safety Briefing

Topic: Tool and Equipment Inspection

---

Situation

Field crews rely on tools and equipment throughout the workday. Over time,
tools can develop defects such as worn insulation, loose components,
damaged handles, or malfunctioning controls that may not be immediately
obvious.

---

Why It Matters

Defective tools significantly increase the risk of injury and equipment
failure. OSHA notes that tools must be maintained in safe condition and
removed from service when defects are identified to prevent accidents and
injuries.

---

Operational Hazards

• Damaged insulation on electrical tools
• Loose or broken tool handles
• Malfunctioning switches or controls
• Excessive wear on cutting tools or equipment
• Using tools for purposes they were not designed for

---

Control Measures

• Inspect tools before each use
• Remove defective tools from service immediately
• Report damaged equipment through proper channels
• Use the correct tool for the task
• Maintain tools according to manufacturer recommendations

---

Relevant Federal Safety Guidance

OSHA requires employers to ensure that hand and power tools are maintained
in safe condition and that defective tools are removed from service.
The National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health emphasizes that
routine inspection and maintenance of tools reduces injury risk and
equipment failure.

---

Supporting Language from PG&E Governing Documents

PG&E states that "nothing is more important to us than safety" and
emphasizes employee accountability in protecting coworkers and the public
through safe work practices.

---

Supporting Language from IBEW 1245 Physical Agreement

The Company and Union affirm that the safety of employees, contractors,

---

Field Safety Reminder

A damaged tool can turn a routine task into a serious injury. Inspect
equipment before use and remove unsafe tools from service.

---

2-Sentence Safety Message

Tools that are damaged or improperly maintained can cause serious injuries.
Inspect tools before use and remove defective equipment from service
immediately.

---

5 Quick Bullet Points
