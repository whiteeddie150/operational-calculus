Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Interruption Recovery – Reset Before You Resume

Situation
Interruptions happen constantly in this line of work:
- radio traffic
- phone calls
- changing assignments
- questions from others
- shifting priorities
- stopping and restarting tasks

Most of the time, the interruption itself is not the problem. The risk
comes from how we restart the work afterward.

Why It Matters
Research across aviation, medicine, industrial operations, and human
performance shows that interruptions increase the likelihood of skipped
steps, missed controls, and assumption-based decisions.

The brain naturally assumes continuity after interruption. People often
restart where they think they left off instead of verifying where they
actually are.

Operational Risk
The interruption usually isn't what creates the failure.
The failure happens when:
- verification is skipped
- assumptions replace confirmation
- the work resumes too quickly
- controls are not rechecked

This is especially dangerous during:
- routine work
- familiar tasks
- time pressure
- end-of-job operations

Connections to Previous Briefs
We've talked before about:
- drift
- verification vs assumption
- task fixation
- cognitive bias
- questioning attitude

Interruptions are one of the ways those problems begin.

An interruption creates an opportunity for:
- drift
- missed verification
- fixation on getting the task finished
- assumptions replacing awareness

Where It Shows Up
In the Field
- stopping mid-sequence
- radio interruptions during setup
- changing conditions during the task
- restarting after walking away

In the Office / Calls
- multitasking during meetings
- long email chains
- changing priorities mid-task
- interrupted planning or review

Behavioral Warning Signs
- "I know where I was"
- restarting too quickly
- skipping rechecks
- relying on memory instead of verification
- rushing to regain tempo

Operational Strength
Strong crews deliberately reset after interruption:
- pause
- reassess
- verify controls
- confirm task position
- communicate before resuming

They don't assume continuity.

Connection to PG&E Standards
PG&E expects a questioning attitude:
- don't assume
- verify
- stop when unsure

That applies after interruptions too.

Field Safety Reminder
After interruption, don't restart where you think you were. Restart by
verifying where you actually are.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Interruptions disrupt more than the task, they disrupt awareness and
memory. Slow down, reset, and verify before resuming the work.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Interruptions create drift
- Don't assume continuity
- Recheck controls after interruption
- Verify task position before resuming
- Slow down before restarting

Core PG&E Safety Philosophy

- Identify catastrophic hazards early
- Verify controls instead of assuming them
- Build capacity for failure recovery
- Encourage questioning and challenge
- Learn from operational reality
- Stop work when controls degrade
- Focus on prevention before exposure occurs
