Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Task Fixation – When Focus Becomes a Risk

Situation
We're trained to focus on the job and get it done right. That focus is a
strength, but it can also become a problem when it narrows our attention
too much.

Why It Matters
Task fixation happens when we become so focused on completing a task that
we stop seeing what's changing around us.
There is a well-known human factor called cognitive lockup, or fixation
error. It's when we stay locked into the task or plan even after conditions
have changed.

What Cognitive Lockup Looks Like
- "I just need to finish this"
- Sticking to the plan even when something feels off
- Ignoring new information or warning signs
- Not reassessing once the job is underway

Operational Hazards
- Losing awareness of surroundings while focused on a task
- Missing changes in conditions (weather, equipment, environment)
- Ignoring warning signs or near misses
- Continuing work even when something feels off
- Failing to reassess once the job is underway

Control Measures
- Pause and reset your awareness during the job
- Look up and scan your surroundings regularly
- Reassess conditions, not just the task
- Listen when someone else raises a concern
- Be willing to stop and step back if something changes

Operational Strength
Strong crews:
- stay aware of the full environment, not just the task
- check in with each other during the job
- recognize when focus is becoming tunnel vision
- reset before conditions force the issue

Stop Work and Awareness
If you stay locked into the task after conditions change, you are no longer
in control.
Stepping back is not losing progress, it's maintaining safety.

Field Safety Reminder
The job in front of you is not the only risk around you.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Focus helps you do the job, but fixation can get you hurt. If conditions
change, step back and reassess.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Don't get locked into the task
- Look up and scan often
- Reassess as the job progresses
- Listen to your crew
- Step back when something feels off
