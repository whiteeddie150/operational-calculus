Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Running Out of Room

Situation
Most jobs start with a buffer, time to work, space from hazards, and a
clear way out. As conditions change, that buffer can shrink without being
recognized.

Why It Matters
Incidents don't usually happen because something suddenly became dangerous.
They happen when we slowly run out of time, space, or options and don't
adjust early enough.
In fire conditions, changing weather, delayed communication, and extended
time on task can reduce that buffer quickly.

Operational Hazards
- Staying on task longer than planned
- Losing distance from hazards due to changing conditions
- Reduced visibility or access
- Delayed or incomplete information
- Fewer escape or repositioning options

Control Measures
- Ask: Do I still have time, space, and a way out?
- Reassess when conditions begin to change
- Confirm who is monitoring conditions for your crew
- Identify escape routes before starting work
- Act early if your buffer starts to shrink
