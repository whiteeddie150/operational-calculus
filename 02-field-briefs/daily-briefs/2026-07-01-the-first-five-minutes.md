MORNING SAFETY BRIEF

Topic: The First Five Minutes

Supporting PG&E Virtue: Owners

Situation

Every task has a beginning.

Whether you're arriving at a transmission tower, unloading equipment,
starting a weed eater, or walking a new section of right-of-way, the first
few minutes often determine how the rest of the job unfolds.

Those first moments are when we establish our work area, identify hazards,
and decide how we will perform the task.

Why It Matters

Research in human performance shows that people form an initial mental
model very quickly. If that first assessment is incomplete or based on
assumptions, we may spend the rest of the task working from an inaccurate
picture.

Taking a few extra moments at the beginning can prevent hours of dealing
with the consequences of a poor start.

Operational Awareness

Before beginning work today, ask yourself:

• What is different from the last site?
• Where are my escape routes?
• What terrain or footing concerns exist?
• Where will I stage my tools?
• What could change while I'm working?

Questioning Attitude

Don't let familiarity replace observation.

You've probably completed hundreds of similar jobs, but this is not
yesterday's job.

Today's conditions deserve today's assessment.

Supporting PG&E Virtue

Owners

Owners don't just start work.

They start work with purpose.

A deliberate beginning often leads to a safer and more efficient finish.

Field Application

Today:

• Walk the work area before beginning.
• Identify unstable footing.
• Stage equipment where it reduces unnecessary movement.
• Remove obvious trip hazards.
• Agree on the work plan with your partner before starting.

Safety Reminder

A rushed start often creates problems that cannot be recovered later.

Take the time to build a strong beginning.

2-Sentence Safety Message

The first five minutes of a task often shape the next five hours. Slow down
just enough to understand today's conditions before committing to the work.

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Pause before beginning
• Scan the entire work area
• Verify footing and escape routes
• Stage tools deliberately
• Start with today's conditions, not yesterday's assumptions

Closing Thought

Professionals don't simply begin work.

They establish the conditions for success before the work truly begins.

SCIENCE & THOUGHT LEADER ADDENDUM

Gary Klein – Recognition-Primed Decision Making

Experienced operators rapidly assess new situations by comparing what they
observe to what they expect. Effective decision-making begins with building
an accurate mental picture before taking action.

Practical Takeaway:
The first assessment is often the most important assessment.

---

James Reason – Human Error

Many operational failures begin with small assumptions made early in a
task. These assumptions can remain unchallenged until they contribute to an
incident.

Practical Takeaway:
Early observations help prevent later mistakes.

---

NIOSH Hazard Recognition

NIOSH emphasizes that identifying hazards before work begins is one of the
most effective ways to reduce workplace injuries.

Practical Takeaway:
Hazard recognition is most valuable before exposure occurs.

---

Human Performance Connection

The beginning of every task is when workers have the greatest flexibility
to change their plan, reposition equipment, or eliminate hazards. As work
progresses, options often become more limited.

Practical Takeaway:
The safest time to improve a job is usually before it begins.
