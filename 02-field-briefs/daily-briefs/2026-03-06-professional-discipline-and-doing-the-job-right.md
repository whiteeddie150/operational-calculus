Tailboard Safety Briefing

Date: Friday, March 6, 2026
Topic: Professional Discipline and Doing the Job Right

---

Situation

By the end of the week, crews have completed many tasks and handled
multiple priorities. Maintaining the same level of discipline, attention to
detail, and safe work practices on Friday is just as important as it was on
Monday.

---

Why It Matters

Many incidents occur when routine tasks are performed without full
attention to procedure. Maintaining professional discipline ensures hazards
are identified, procedures are followed, and the work is completed safely
and correctly.

---

Operational Hazards

• Rushing through routine tasks
• Skipping procedural steps because the job feels familiar
• Incomplete checks on tools, equipment, or work areas
• Assumptions about conditions instead of verification
• Reduced communication during final task execution

---

Control Measures

• Follow established procedures for every task
• Take the time to verify conditions before acting
• Perform equipment and site checks with the same care as earlier in the
week
• Communicate clearly with crew members during operations
• Maintain focus and professionalism through the final task

---

Relevant Federal Safety Guidance

Effective safety programs rely on consistent adherence to procedures and
hazard recognition throughout all operations. Clear communication and
attention to detail reduce the likelihood of workplace incidents.

---

Supporting Language from PG&E Governing Documents

PG&E emphasizes that safety is the foundation of its operations and that
employees are accountable for protecting coworkers and the public.

---

Supporting Language from IBEW 1245 Physical Agreement

The Company and Union affirm that safety is the highest priority and that
both parties cooperate to maintain safe working conditions.

---

Field Safety Reminder

Professional discipline means doing the job right every time. Stay focused,
follow the process, and leave the work knowing it was done safely and
correctly.

---

2-Sentence Version
