Tailboard Safety Briefing

Date: Monday, March 9, 2026
Topic: Daylight Saving Time and Sleep Disruption

---

Situation

Daylight Saving Time began yesterday, and most people lost one hour of
sleep. Even small disruptions to sleep patterns can affect alertness,
reaction time, and concentration during the first few days after the time
change.

---

Why It Matters

Sleep disruption can reduce cognitive performance and increase the
likelihood of errors during routine tasks. Maintaining focus and
recognizing fatigue during the adjustment period helps prevent incidents
and protects both workers and the public.

---

Operational Hazards

• Reduced alertness due to sleep disruption
• Slower reaction time during routine tasks
• Reduced concentration during equipment operation
• Irritability or reduced communication among crew members
• Increased likelihood of minor mistakes

---

Control Measures

• Allow extra time to fully wake up and prepare before work
• Stay hydrated and maintain proper nutrition
• Take short breaks if focus begins to drop
• Communicate clearly and verify instructions during tasks
• Be mindful of coworkers who may also be adjusting to the time change

---

Relevant Statistics from Federal Data

Research has shown that sleep disruption following the transition to
Daylight Saving Time can temporarily affect alertness and increase
fatigue-related risks in the days immediately following the time change.

Field Safety Reminder

The time change may only cost an hour of sleep, but it can affect focus for
several days. Stay aware, communicate clearly, and give yourself time to
fully adjust.

---

2-Sentence Safety Message

Daylight Saving Time started yesterday, which means most people lost an
