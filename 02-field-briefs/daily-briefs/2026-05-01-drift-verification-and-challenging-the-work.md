Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Positive Friday – Drift, Verification, and Challenging the Work

Situation
We tend to think problems show up at the jobsite. Most of the time, they
don't start there. They start earlier in planning, assumptions,
communication, or decisions that never get challenged.

Why It Matters
PG&E expects a questioning attitude. That means we don't assume, we
verify.
This line of work doesn't fail all at once, it drifts. Small things go
uncorrected, and the work slowly moves away from where it should be.

Where It Shows Up
- Plans that don't quite make sense but move forward
- Briefings that leave gaps
- Assumptions that go unchallenged
- Conditions that change but the plan doesn't
- Quick agreement without real verification

Off the Jobsite
Drift doesn't just happen in the field:
- In the office: decisions move forward without being fully challenged
- On calls: the same voices talk while others stay quiet
- In email: questions go unanswered and concerns get softened

If it's not being challenged there, it won't get corrected before it
reaches the field.

Operational Risk
Most problems aren't missed because nobody saw them.
They're missed because nobody questioned or corrected them.

When we don't verify, we're not in control.

Operational Strength
Strong crews don't just execute the work, they verify it:
- Question what doesn't make sense
- Check conditions against the plan
- Speak up early when something is off
- Stop and reset when unsure

Connection to the Standard
The expectation is clear:
- maintain a questioning attitude
- verify before proceeding
- stop when unsure

Field Safety Reminder
If the work isn't being challenged, it's drifting.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Don't assume the job is right, verify it. If something doesn't line up,
question it before you continue.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Don't assume, verify
- Question what doesn't make sense
- Check conditions against the plan
- Stop when unsure
- If you see it, say it
