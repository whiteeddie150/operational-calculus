Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Drift and Correction – Who's Challenging the Work?

Situation
We tend to think problems show up at the jobsite. Most of the time, they
don't start there. They start earlier, in planning, assumptions,
communication, or decisions that never get challenged.

Why It Matters
This line of work doesn't fail all at once. It drifts. Small things go
uncorrected, questions don't get asked, and the work slowly moves away from
where it should be.
By the time it shows up in the field, it's already been off for a while.

Where Drift Happens
- Plans that don't quite make sense
- Briefings that leave gaps
- Assumptions that go unchallenged
- Conditions that change but the plan doesn't
- "We've always done it this way" thinking

What It Looks Like
- Something feels off, but nobody says it
- The room gets quiet when it should get loud
- People push through instead of stepping back
- Everyone assumes someone else checked it

Operational Risk
It's rarely that nobody saw the problem.
It's that nobody corrected it.

Every bad outcome has a moment where the work could've been challenged or
stopped. That moment only matters if someone says it out loud.

Operational Strength
Strong crews don't just do the work, they correct it:
- They question what doesn't make sense
- They speak up early
- They adjust when conditions change
- They don't let things drift

This applies everywhere:
- in planning
- in the briefing
- in the field
- after the job starts

Field Safety Reminder
If the work isn't being challenged, it's drifting.

2-Sentence Safety Message
This work stays safe because we correct it as we go, not because it starts
safe. If you see something off, say it early.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Question what doesn't make sense
- Don't let assumptions go unchecked
- Speak up early, not late
- Adjust when conditions change
- If you see it, own it
