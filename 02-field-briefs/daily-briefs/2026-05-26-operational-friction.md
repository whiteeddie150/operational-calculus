Tuesday Safety Brief
Topic: Operational Friction – Small Problems Add Up

Situation
Most operational problems do not begin as major failures.

More often, they begin as small forms of operational friction:
- unclear communication
- interruptions
- changing priorities
- equipment issues
- delayed information
- radio congestion
- repeated task switching
- environmental stress
- incomplete pass-downs

None of these problems seem significant individually.

The challenge is how they quietly accumulate over time.

Why It Matters
Operational friction consumes attention and awareness.

As friction builds:
- communication shortens
- frustration increases
- assumptions spread
- verification weakens
- attention narrows
- situational awareness degrades

The operation usually does not become unsafe all at once.

Small unresolved problems slowly begin driving the pace and direction of
the operation.

Operational Reality
Dynamic operations naturally contain friction.

Campaign-fire environments especially create:
- changing conditions
- information lag
- compressed communication
- environmental stress
- overlapping priorities
- incomplete operational pictures

Strong crews recognize friction early before it begins controlling the
operation.

Connections to Previous Briefs
We've talked before about:
- drift
- weak signals
- tempo vs awareness
- interruption recovery
- crew cross-checking
- information lag

Operational friction feeds all of them.

The more friction accumulates:
- the easier it becomes to miss changing conditions
- the harder it becomes to maintain deliberate awareness
- the more likely assumptions replace verification

Where It Shows Up

In the Field
- repeated interruptions
- changing assignments
- unclear directions
- equipment issues
- access problems
- radio overload
- environmental exposure

In the Office / Coordination Environment
- fragmented communication
- task saturation
- rapid context switching
- incomplete updates
- conflicting priorities
- overloaded information flow

Behavioral Warning Signs
- frustration driving decisions
- rushing to "catch up"
- reduced questioning
- shortened communication
- scattered attention
- assumption-based problem solving
- mental overload

Operational Strength
Strong crews:
- recognize friction early
- slow down enough to reassess
- clarify communication
- reset priorities when needed
- cross-check assumptions
- prevent small problems from compounding

They maintain operational control instead of reacting emotionally to tempo
and friction.

Connection to PG&E Standards and Doctrine

This supports:
- questioning attitude
- verification over assumption
- operational awareness
- SIF prevention
- operational learning
- Human and Organizational Performance (HOP)

PG&E doctrine recognizes that work is:
"complex and dynamic"

Strong operations recognize and manage friction before it degrades
awareness and control.

Field Safety Reminder
Small operational problems have a way of stacking together. Strong crews
recognize friction early before it starts driving the operation.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Operational friction quietly consumes awareness, communication quality, and
decision-making capacity over time. Strong crews maintain control by
recognizing small problems early and preventing them from compounding.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Small problems add up
- Friction consumes awareness
- Clarify communication early
- Verify instead of assuming
- Maintain control under pressure
