Tailboard Safety Briefing

Date: February 24, 2026
Topic: Complacency in Routine Tasks

---

Situation

Many of our daily tasks are routine. Familiar routes, repeated procedures,
and well-known equipment can create a false sense of security that leads to
reduced attention and shortcut behavior.

---

Why It Matters

Most workplace incidents occur during routine operations, not complex
emergencies. When attention drifts and risks are normalized, hazards go
unrecognized and small deviations compound into serious incidents.

---

Operational Hazards

• Skipping hazard assessments because the task is routine
• Taking shortcuts to save time
• Reduced situational awareness during familiar work
• Overconfidence leading to missed warning signs
• Failure to reassess changing conditions

---

Control Measures

• Conduct a quick hazard scan before starting any task
• Follow procedures every time, even when the job is familiar
• Encourage crew members to speak up when something looks different
• Pause and reassess when conditions change
• Use stop-work authority when risk increases

---

Relevant Statistics from Federal Data

Human factors, including complacency and inattention, are major

Field Safety Reminder

Routine work is where complacency lives. Stay present, follow the process,
and speak up when something feels off. Familiar does not mean risk-free.
