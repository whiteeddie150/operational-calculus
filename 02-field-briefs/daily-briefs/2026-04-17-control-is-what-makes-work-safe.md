Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Positive Friday – Control is What Makes Work Safe

Situation
Every task we perform involves some level of hazard. What makes the job
safe is not the absence of risk, it's the presence of effective control.
The strength of our operation is defined by how well we identify and manage
those hazards.

Why It Matters
The most effective way to prevent injury is to control the hazard, not just
protect against it. Safety systems use the Hierarchy of Controls, which
prioritizes eliminating hazards first and relying on PPE last.
When we rely only on PPE, we are already operating at the lowest level of
control.

Operational Hazards
- Relying on PPE as the primary control
- Accepting hazards instead of eliminating them
- Temporary fixes becoming permanent conditions
- Failure to reassess controls as conditions change
- Continuing work when controls are no longer effective

Control Measures (Hierarchy of Controls)
- Elimination: Remove the hazard entirely whenever possible
- Substitution: Replace the hazard with a safer option
- Engineering Controls: Isolate people from the hazard (guards, barriers)
- Administrative Controls: Change how the work is performed (procedures,
training)
- PPE: Protect the worker when other controls are not sufficient

Operational Strength
- Prioritizing elimination over protection
- Fixing the problem, not just working around it
- Using interim controls while permanent solutions are developed
- Verifying controls are working and not creating new hazards
- Involving the crew in identifying and improving controls

Stop Work and Control
If a hazard is not controlled, the job is not ready to be done.
Stopping work is not failure, it is the correct response when control is
lost.

Field Safety Reminder
We don't get safe outcomes by chance. We get them by controlling the hazard.

2-Sentence Safety Message
Safety is built by controlling hazards, not just reacting to them. The
stronger the control, the safer the outcome.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Eliminate hazards whenever possible
- Don't rely on PPE as your primary control
- Fix the problem, don't work around it
- Reassess when conditions change
- If control is lost, stop and reassess
