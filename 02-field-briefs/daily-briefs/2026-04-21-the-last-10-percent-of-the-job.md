Team,

Morning Safety Brief
Topic: The Last 10% of the Job

Situation
Most of the job gets done without issue. The setup is good, the work is
controlled, and everything goes as planned. Then something happens at the
end, during cleanup, final adjustments, or "just finishing up."

Why It Matters
A large number of injuries don't happen at the start of the job, they
happen at the end. When the task feels complete, attention drops, shortcuts
creep in, and control starts to fade.
The job isn't done when the main work is complete. It's done when
everything is put away, secured, and everyone is clear.

Operational Hazards
- Rushing to finish and move on
- Skipping steps during cleanup
- Letting guards or controls slip at the end
- Working tired or mentally checked out
- Handling tools or materials casually

What Happens in the Last 10%
- Focus shifts from doing the job to being done
- People assume the risk is over
- Small shortcuts start to feel acceptable
- Situational awareness drops

Control Measures
- Treat the last 10% like the first 10%
- Slow down when the job is almost done
- Maintain control of tools and materials
- Keep PPE and protections in place until fully complete
- Take a final look before walking away

Operational Strength
Strong crews:
- finish the job the same way they started it
- don't rush the last step
- stay engaged all the way through
- recognize that the end of the job is still part of the job

Stop Work and Control
If control starts to slip at the end, reset it.
Being "almost done" is not a reason to take risk.

Field Safety Reminder
Most jobs don't go wrong at the beginning. They go wrong when we think
they're already over.

2-Sentence Safety Message
The last 10% of the job carries real risk. Stay focused and finish with the
same discipline you started with.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Don't rush the finish
- Stay focused until the job is fully done
- Keep controls in place through cleanup
- Watch for shortcuts at the end
- Finish strong, not fast
