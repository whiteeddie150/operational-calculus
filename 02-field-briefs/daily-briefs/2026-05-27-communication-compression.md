Morning Safety Brief
Topic: Communication Compression – When Tempo Shortens Communication

Situation
As operational tempo increases, communication naturally becomes shorter.

That happens in:
- campaign-fire environments
- field operations
- switching operations
- storm response
- EOC coordination
- routine high-workload periods

People begin:
- shortening explanations
- skipping clarification
- assuming shared understanding
- relying on shorthand
- compressing pass-downs

That is a normal human response to workload and tempo.

The problem is that compressed communication can quietly degrade
operational awareness.

Why It Matters
Strong operations depend on crews sharing the same operational picture.

As communication compresses:
- details disappear
- assumptions spread
- misunderstandings increase
- verification weakens
- situational awareness fragments

Most of the time, nobody realizes operational understanding has already
started diverging.

Operational Reality
Dynamic operations naturally pressure communication systems.

Campaign-fire environments especially create:
- radio congestion
- changing priorities
- incomplete information
- multitasking
- environmental stress
- compressed timelines

Under pressure, communication often shifts from:
- deliberate clarification
to
- assumption-based understanding

That is where operational alignment begins degrading.

Connections to Previous Briefs
We've talked before about:
- tempo vs awareness
- information lag
- drift
- operational friction
- crew cross-checking
- verification vs assumption

Communication compression feeds all of them.

As tempo increases:
- communication shortens
- reassessment decreases
- assumptions last longer
- operational pictures begin separating

Where It Shows Up

In the Field
- rushed radio traffic
- incomplete hazard communication
- abbreviated pass-downs
- "copy" replacing confirmation
- assumption-based direction
- skipped clarification

In Coordination Environments
- fragmented updates
- incomplete operational context
- rapid task switching
- overloaded communication channels
- assumption carryover between operational periods

Behavioral Warning Signs
- shortened explanations
- "you know what I mean"
- incomplete confirmation
- reduced questioning
- rushed pass-downs
- assumption-based understanding
- communication gaps during workload spikes

Operational Strength
Strong crews deliberately protect communication quality during high-tempo
operations.

They:
- clarify early
- repeat critical details
- verify understanding
- challenge ambiguity
- close communication loops
- slow down enough to maintain shared awareness

Strong crews understand:
fast communication is not always clear communication.

Connection to PG&E Standards and Doctrine

This supports:
- questioning attitude
- verification over assumption
- operational awareness
- SIF prevention
- operational learning
- Human and Organizational Performance (HOP)

PG&E doctrine emphasizes:
- accurate information flow
- questioning assumptions
- maintaining awareness during dynamic operations

Communication quality is one of the controls that helps preserve
operational alignment during changing conditions.

Field Safety Reminder
Communication usually gets shorter right before awareness starts degrading.
Protect clarity before assumptions begin replacing understanding.

2-Sentence Safety Message
As operational tempo increases, communication naturally compresses and
details begin disappearing. Strong crews deliberately protect clarity and
shared understanding before assumptions start driving the operation.

5 Quick Bullet Points
- Tempo naturally shortens communication
- Assumptions grow inside unclear communication
- Clarify early before confusion compounds
- Verify understanding, not just acknowledgment
- Shared awareness depends on communication quality
