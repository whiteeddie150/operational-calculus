MORNING SAFETY BRIEF

Topic: Information Lag

Supporting PG&E Virtue: Trustworthy

Situation

As fire season begins to accelerate, many of us will find ourselves working
in and around dynamic environments where conditions can change rapidly. One
of the less visible hazards in these environments is information lag.

Information lag occurs when conditions change faster than our understanding
of those conditions.

The information may not be wrong. It may simply no longer be current.

Why It Matters

Many operational problems begin when decisions are made using information
that was accurate earlier in the day but no longer reflects current
conditions.

Weather changes.

Fire behavior changes.

Access routes change.

Resource assignments change.

Operational priorities change.

The longer we operate without verifying assumptions, the greater the chance
that our understanding begins to drift away from reality.

Operational Awareness

Strong operators continuously ask:

• What has changed?
• Is my information still current?
• Who else needs this information?
• Am I acting on verified information or an assumption?

These questions help prevent outdated information from becoming operational
risk.

Field Application

For those operating near active incidents, remember that conditions outside
your immediate work area can influence your safety.

Information delays can occur between operational changes and when those
changes become widely known.

Do not assume that what was true at the start of the shift remains true
later in the day.

Take time to verify changing conditions, communicate updates, and challenge
assumptions that may no longer be valid.

Supporting PG&E Virtue

Trustworthy

Operationally, trustworthiness means communicating what you know, what you
do not know, and what has changed.

Teams build trust when information moves quickly, accurately, and
completely.

Trustworthy operators help maintain a common understanding of conditions
and reduce the risk created by outdated assumptions.

Safety Reminder

Information has a shelf life.

Verify before acting. Communicate before assumptions take hold.

2-Sentence Safety Message

Information lag occurs when conditions change faster than our understanding
of those conditions. Verifying information and communicating changes early
helps keep the team aligned with reality and reduces unnecessary risk.

5 Quick Bullet Points

• Verify information before acting
• Ask what has changed since the last update
• Challenge assumptions created by stale information
• Communicate changing conditions early
• Help maintain a common operating picture

understanding still matches current conditions.
