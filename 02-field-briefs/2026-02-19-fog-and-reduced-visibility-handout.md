Reduced Visibility and Fog Conditions
Field Safety Handout

Why This Matters

Fog, low clouds, and heavy mist reduce visibility and depth perception,
increasing the risk of vehicle incidents and delayed hazard recognition.
Limited sight distance affects braking time, route decisions, and crew
safety during response and routine travel.

Key Statistics (U.S.)

Fog contributes to over 38,000 crashes annually.

These crashes result in more than 600 deaths and 16,000 injuries each year.

Nearly half of fog-related crashes occur during early morning hours when
visibility is lowest.

Multi-vehicle pileups are more common in dense fog due to sudden speed
changes.
