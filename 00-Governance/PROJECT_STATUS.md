# Project Status

## Scope

This project develops safety and operational-awareness content for high-consequence field work — fire, EMS, and utility operations. It is not a general theory of human participation in systems.

## What exists

65 daily safety briefs recovered from the Gmail sent folder, spanning January through July 2026, indexed in `02-field-briefs/` (`02-field-briefs/daily-briefs/README.md` for the day-to-day briefs; `02-field-briefs/README.md` for the standalone handout and annex material).

## What has been checked

A theme analysis (`02-field-briefs/THEME_ANALYSIS.md`) compares the 65 briefs against named research: Conklin, Graham, Weick and Sutcliffe, Klein, Boyd, and Kegan and Lahey. Conclusion: the briefs are a grounded synthesis and occupational translation of existing research, not a new theory. One thread — the Rattlesnake Fire brief and the modern multi-agency communication gap it points to (`03-case-studies/rattlesnake-fire-communication-lag.md`) — is flagged as the strongest candidate for anything beyond that synthesis, and it remains undeveloped.

## Ongoing delivery

Brief-writing and archiving going forward follow `00-Governance/DELIVERY_STRUCTURE.md`.

## Naming

The project's name is intentionally undecided, pending further development of the work itself.
